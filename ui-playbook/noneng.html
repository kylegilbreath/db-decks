UI Contribution Playbook — For Non-Engineers
Databricks Design · 2026

Why this matters

  • Research and Figma prototypes die in documentation or land at low fidelity.
  • AI-assisted PRs can blow past review norms and create technical debt.
  • Without a shared language between eng and non-eng, effort gets wasted and architectural debt builds quietly.
Here's what we're trying.

A system that lets non-engineers engage, prototype, and create using plans and living specs — while holding engineers accountable for design, too.

Non-engineers
Provide a functional spec — a code PoC or a plan.md.
Engineers
Provide technical stability and design accountability.

Four paths to production

Path Best for Your job Outcome
Direct Fix Nits, text, CSS tweaks Standard PR + before/after screenshot Production

The handshake

  • Post in Slack before you start — share your path, intent, and expected scope. 30 seconds upfront beats two weeks of back-and-forth at review.
  • For Plan-Build: get plan.md sign-off before writing production code. Alignment is free. Rework is expensive.
  • If a Direct Fix grows in scope: stop, open a draft PR, post to Slack. An engineer will triage within one business day.

Shared standards

  • Every PR needs a Jira ticket — no ghost PRs. Tickets link back to Figma, research docs, Slack threads.
  • Attach a screenshot or screen recording to every visual PR. No media = no review. This is non-negotiable.
  • You're encouraged to give UI feedback on engineers' PRs too. Usability and UI are everyone's responsibility, not just a designer nicety.
Before you touch
the codebase:
Pick a path. Share intent. Get alignment. Then build. That 30-second Slack message is the difference between a fast merge and a two-week rework.
